The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill is an album with a Masterful blend
of R&B, Hip-Hop, Reggae, Blues, and traces of other genres. Released
in 1998 during the 90s golden age of hip-hop Lauryn Hill took her
musical experience and expertise shown in her previous work with the
Fugees and takes it to a whole new level.

Favorite Tracks: Ex Factor, Nothing Even Matters, Tell Him, To Zion, Can't Take my eyes off of you







Casiopeia is a Japanese Jazz Fusion band that formed in 1976 with 4 intial
members:

  • Guitarist: Issei Noro
  • Bassist: Tetsuo Sakurai
  • Drummer: Tohru Suzuki
  • Keyboardist: Hidehiko Koike

As a group, even with periodic member changes, Casiopeia has remained a vital
standpoint for jazz fusion bands and the genre as a whole. They are the only band
that I personally listen to live recordings of as they normally have a great amount
amount of energy in their playing and are overall fantastic.

From their self titled album my fav tracks are: Track 2, Track 3




















Music Not On Major Streaming Services


There is a lot of music that is unavailable on streaming services. Either due to licensing issues, artist preference, or no official release.
One of the major sites with this music is YouTube. Here are some examples of albums not on any major streaming service.






NOTE: Embeded YouTube videos are 3 figures set inline with figcaptions on each


The three albums above are fantastic, and each showcase one of my reasons for it not being on
streaming services. Masayoshi Takanaka's The Rainbow Goblines is only available on YouTube along with
some of his other albums due to restrictions of his record label or those projects.
Meanwhile, Chloe Burbank is a realease by Joji before he had retired from being Filthy Frank
on YouTube. Since this was a self released album under no label it has not been
put onto major streaming platforms. And finally Kanye West's project Yandhi. It was an album
set to release on September 29, 2018, but after the date had passed he just never released it.
Now it exists as a sort of mixtape perpetuated by fans who use leaked versions of the tracks
as the material. Because of this process you can find many versions of Yandhi as well. All at
different points of the album being created and in different track combinations.
