Film is a very interesting medium compared to music as it holds
music itself and a visual aspect which is primary. Soundtracks in
film have evolved immensely throughout its history, with the first
films not even having sound! The three movies shown above each have
interesting uses of sound, the most interesting soundtrack of them being Kill Bill's.

In Kill Bill, Quentin Tarantino finds a unique way to blend the western
style of music with the various other aspects of the movie, with a tinge
of eastern influence during the portions that take place in Japan, and
full buckled down western when the movie moves towards the heart of America.




Visuals in Film


The great ability of film is the combination of both visual and audio mediums.
This allows for creative capabilities unlike any other medium since it contains
both combined. In film many have masterfully done this combination to create very
desired emotions or aesthetics as well as entire fictional worlds with immense depth.
