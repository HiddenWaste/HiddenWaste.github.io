Now to talk about more revolutions that have taken place in
our world. I'll talk about 3 revolutions that each show one of:

  • Revenge
  • Radicalism
  • Redemption





Radicalism


Revolutions as a result of radicalism tend to take a very different form
than that of those born out of civil unrest. Most of the time it's a slow
change that takes place in the upper echelons of a country's government.
An easy example of a revolution of radicalism would be Nazi Germany, showcasing
how an ideology can take root in a government, bleed to the people, and
push forward destructive behaviors and the bidding of one man.



Redemption


Redemption is a more difficult motive to pin down for revolution as most
revolutions have this as a part of the reason. Be it redemption of the
poor in bringing down the upper class or like with the revenge example a
group of people reclaiming what was taken from them to return themselves to
what they had before and feel redeemed.

The Example I will point towards will be most if not all of the South American wars of independence
as they fought to remove the Spanish occupancy and return independence to th nations
even though by this point many of the people had spaniard heritage and in some countries
the indigenous people were treated poorly.

Revenge


A great example of a revolution based out of revenge is the
Ako incident in Japan. Also known as the revenge of the 47
ronin, it is an event in which people enacted revenge on the
man who usurped power by killing their master. This story could
also be used to showcase redemption as that's one of the many factors
which fueled their revenge campaign.
